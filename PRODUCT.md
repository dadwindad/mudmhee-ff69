# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Thai Mudmee (ikat) pattern designers spanning all skill levels and roles — artisan weavers, fashion designers, textile researchers, academics, and students. Primary usage context is focused creative work on an iPad Air 5 (1180×820pt), often seated at a table or studio, in sustained sessions. Users expect a tool that respects both the technical complexity of crystallographic symmetry and the cultural richness of the craft.

## Product Purpose

Mudmee Studio is a digital atelier for Thai ikat pattern design. It lets users draw motifs by hand or on a pixel grid, apply symmetry groups (wallpaper for body, frieze for border), assemble body + border into a full fabric layout, and preview the result wrapped on a 3D ผ้าถุง form. Designs can be saved to a local gallery, exported as PNG or JSON, and reloaded for iteration. Success looks like: a weaver or designer completes a full pattern from sketch to 3D preview without friction, and feels proud of the output.

## Brand Personality

Creative, Modern, Cultural — a professional creative tool that feels alive and iPad-native, like Procreate crossed with Canva, but rooted in Thai textile heritage. Warm without being ornamental. Confident without being intimidating. Bilingual (EN/TH) as a first-class feature, not an afterthought.

## Anti-references

- **Adobe Illustrator**: heavy panel system, menu-dense, desktop-only feel — avoid deep nesting of tools and modal dialogs
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: navy/blue/gray enterprise apps that feel sterile and Western — this is a cultural creative tool
- **Current stone/rose Tailwind default look**: the existing UI is functional but visually generic; the redesign must feel intentional and art-directed
- **Skeuomorphic "canvas" apps**: fake paper textures, faux wood panels — keep it flat, crisp, and digital

## Design Principles

1. **Canvas first** — the motif editor and pattern preview are the product; UI chrome should recede and never compete with the work
2. **Touch-native** — all interactions designed for finger and Apple Pencil on a 10.9" screen; 44pt minimum touch targets everywhere
3. **Cultural confidence** — Thai script, Thai terminology, and pattern aesthetics are first-class citizens, not translated afterthoughts
4. **Progressive disclosure** — show the simple path first; reveal symmetry math and advanced controls when the user reaches for them
5. **Light/dark as craft setting** — dark mode for focused drawing sessions, light mode for review and export; both themes must be fully art-directed, not just color-inverted

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline; 4.5:1 body text contrast minimum in both themes
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44pt for all interactive elements (iPad primary)
- Reduced-motion alternative for all animations
- Thai and English labels on all controls
- Color palette must remain legible for common forms of color blindness (no red/green-only distinctions for state)
